Description
I designed the Automated Rotary Turntable to streamline part inspection and sorting with minimal manual involvement. Parts are automatically distributed across eight bins, allowing operators to focus on other tasks or manage multiple machines. The turntable is driven using spur gears and a shaft, with the table mounted on camshafts. Everything is fabricated from custom sheet metal and CNC-machined components for precision and durability. Proximity sensors control the indexing, smoothly moving from bin to bin as each fills. This design keeps parts organized, oil-free, and ensures a clean, efficient workflow.

  • Advanced the concept into a fully manufacturable design, including:
    • Sheet metal and machining drawings.
    • Bill of materials for purchased components.
    • Detailed welding, assembly, and powder coating drawings.
  • Led a competitive bidding process to secure the most economical and quality suppliers.
  • Coordinated closely with suppliers of CNC machining, laser cutting, and powder coating to ensure high-quality production.
  • Managed welding, electrical, and assembly teams to ensure smooth project execution.
  • Conducted thorough testing and troubleshooting to confirm operational reliability.
